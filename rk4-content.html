Theory Behind RK4

What is the Runge-Kutta 4 Method?

The Runge-Kutta 4 (RK4) method is a widely used numerical technique for solving ordinary differential equations (ODEs). It provides a balance between computational efficiency and accuracy, making it one of the most popular choices for numerical integration.

General form of first-order ODE:

\[ \frac{dy}{dt} = f(t, y), \quad y(t_0) = y_0 \]

RK4 Algorithm

The RK4 method approximates the solution by calculating four intermediate slopes (k₁ to k₄) and combining them:

Slope calculations:

\[ k_1 = f(t_n, y_n) \]

\[ k_2 = f\left(t_n + \frac{h}{2}, y_n + \frac{h}{2}k_1\right) \]

\[ k_3 = f\left(t_n + \frac{h}{2}, y_n + \frac{h}{2}k_2\right) \]

\[ k_4 = f(t_n + h, y_n + h k_3) \]

Next value:

\[ y_{n+1} = y_n + \frac{h}{6}(k_1 + 2k_2 + 2k_3 + k_4) \]

Where:

\( h \) = step size

\( t_{n+1} = t_n + h \)
